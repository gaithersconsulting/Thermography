Forensic Investigation Services

Specialized in qualitative thermal imaging, we use thermal imaging technology to analyze and diagnose the causes of failures or problems in various structures or systems after an incident or event has occurred. This process is critical for identifying the root causes of issues, documenting evidence, and supporting legal or insurance claims.

Incident Analysis

Fire Investigations: Infrared thermography can be used to assess the spread of heat and fire within a structure, helping to determine the point of origin and understand how the fire progressed. This is crucial for identifying potential causes and responsibilities in fire incidents.

Electrical Failures: After an electrical failure, such as a short circuit or equipment malfunction, thermography can help pinpoint areas of excessive heat that indicate the source of the problem, even after the event.

Water and Moisture Intrusion

Leak Detection: After a flood, storm, or plumbing failure, forensic thermography can help identify areas where water has penetrated building materials. This is important for assessing the extent of damage and planning remediation efforts.

Mold Growth: By identifying moisture-laden areas, thermography aids in detecting conditions that could lead to mold growth, even before mold becomes visible. This is often part of the forensic investigation into indoor air quality issues.

Structural Integrity Assessment

Post-Event Damage Assessment: Following events like earthquakes, explosions, or structural failures, thermography can be used to assess the integrity of building materials and structures. It helps in identifying hidden damage such as delamination, cracks, or voids that are not visible through conventional inspection methods.

Building Envelope Failures: Infrared thermography can detect areas of heat loss, air leaks, or moisture ingress that compromise the building envelope, which is essential in forensic investigations related to building performance and failure.

Forensic investigations using infrared thermography are a critical tool in diagnosing, documenting, and understanding the causes and consequences of various failures or defects in buildings, electrical systems, and other structures. This process not only aids in resolving current issues but also helps prevent future problems by providing detailed, non-invasive insight into the underlying conditions that contribute to failures.

Quality Assurance Investigations

Construction Defects: If there are disputes or concerns about the quality of construction, thermography can be used to identify defects such as poor insulation, thermal bridging, or improper sealing that may not meet building standards.

Verification of Repairs: In forensic contexts, thermography can confirm whether repairs were performed correctly or if they effectively addressed the identified issues.

Legal and Insurance Support

Evidence Documentation: Thermal images can serve as crucial evidence in legal cases or insurance claims by visually demonstrating the presence of defects, damage, or failures. This documentation is often vital in settling disputes or claims.

Expert Testimony: A certified thermographer might be called upon to provide expert testimony in legal proceedings, offering insights based on thermal imaging evidence.

Historical and Preservation Investigations

Assessment of Historic Buildings: Forensic thermography can be used to assess older buildings for hidden issues like moisture intrusion, heat loss, or structural integrity concerns, aiding in preservation efforts and ensuring that any restorations are appropriately planned.
